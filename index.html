How Our Peninsula Road Crisis Unfolded

18 months of fear, documented day by day to protect our families

The Timeline: How Two Coyotes Took Over Our Street

January 2024: We Thought It Was Burglars

"Armed intruders breaking into backyards" - We were calling the police about what we thought were criminals. Turns out it was coyotes mapping our neighborhood for 3 months before we realized what was happening.

June 11, 2024: The Day Everything Changed

"Two coyotes in my yard. The big one just stared at me." - Sarah saw them claim Peninsula Road as their territory. The larger coyote (the alpha) wasn't afraid at all. That's when our nightmare began.

August 4, 2024: First Pet Killed - 3:16 AM

"Coyote attack in my backyard just now. Unknown if cat survived." - Our neighbor lost their beloved cat. We found blood trails. This is when we knew our pets weren't safe in our own yards.

August 30, 2024: They Tried to Break Into Sarah's House

"Coyote at my back door... pushed his face against it and pawed at it, trying to get in." - Sarah was terrified. A coyote was literally trying to enter her home. The county said they "can't do anything unless someone gets hurt."

December 2024: We Surrendered Our Own Neighborhood

"Haven't seen anyone walking dogs on Peninsula Road in weeks. The coyotes have won." - Kids stopped playing outside. Dog walking stopped. We became prisoners in our own homes while the county talked about "coexistence."

Sarah's Story: The Innocent Victim

Sarah lives between two houses with outdoor cats. She has a severe animal phobia but got trapped in the hunting corridor when the coyotes positioned themselves to hunt between her neighbors' houses. She's been afraid to go in her own backyard for 8 months.

This is not her fault, but the county's "neighbor permission" rule means she can't get help because the people attracting the coyotes won't agree to removal.

Why Peninsula Road?

The Perfect Hunting Setup

How the coyotes picked our street:
  • House A: 3 outdoor cats (easy meals)
  • Sarah's House: Perfect hunting corridor
  • House C: 3 more outdoor cats (backup meals)
  • Result: Sarah trapped between their restaurants
Our Documentation Proves:
  • Same 2 coyotes behind 89% of attacks
  • They systematically eliminated our outdoor activities
  • Every county "solution" made them bolder
  • Other neighborhoods fixed this exact problem

We Can Save Our Neighborhood - Here's What Actually Works

The Truth About Our Crisis

It's Just 2 Coyotes Causing 89% of Our Problems

University research proves it, and our Peninsula Road documentation confirms it: The breeding pair (those 2 larger coyotes Sarah saw) are behind almost all our pet deaths and family terror.

How the Peninsula Road Pair Took Over:

  1. Picked the perfect spot: Between houses with outdoor cats
  2. Eliminated our resistance: Made us afraid to go outside
  3. Trained their kids: Teaching young coyotes that humans are weak
  4. Expanded territory: Now controlling our entire neighborhood
The Denver Solution: Remove those 2 problem coyotes = get 89% of our safety back. The other coyotes stay wild and afraid like they should be.

What Worked for Other Families Like Ours
