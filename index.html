The Breeding Pair Solution

Evidence-based strategy that achieves both human safety and wildlife conservation

Scientific Foundation

The 89% Rule

University research confirms: 89% of sheep predation attributed to breeding pairs. Belvedere case study validates this finding in urban environment.

Breeding Pair Strategy:

  1. Territory Selection: Position between multiple prey sources
  2. Hunting Coordination: Alpha pair leads, pack follows
  3. Human Intimidation: Eliminate territorial competition
  4. Resource Monopolization: Control entire hunting corridor
Solution: Remove breeding pair = disrupt 89% of predation activity while preserving non-reproductive animals and maintaining population stability.

Historical Precedent

Pacific Coast indigenous tribes maintained ecological balance for millennia through strategic intervention during pupping season - no population extirpation, effective human-wildlife coexistence.
